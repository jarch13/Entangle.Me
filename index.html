Resonance — entanglement-inspired soulmate demo

A lightweight, link-shareable interactive that teaches: correlation ≠ communication, and correlations degrade with “noise”.

What you’re seeing (user-friendly + professor-safe)

This demo does not claim humans are literally quantum-entangled. Instead, it uses the logic entanglement is inferred by in physics: you don’t observe entanglement directly — you infer it from persistent, non-classical correlations.

  • Entanglement (concept): a joint description where outcomes are best explained relationally, via correlation patterns.
  • What it can do: create correlations that are hard to mimic classically; enable verification/tamper-evidence.
  • What it can’t do: transmit a controllable message (each side still looks random on its own).
  • Decoherence: correlations degrade when the “environment” (noise/interference) increases.

In this demo, you (User A) are compared against a pool of candidates. One candidate is secretly the “linked” partner (strong correlation). Others are baseline (chance correlation). As trials accumulate, the linked partner rises.

Show the “physics translation” of the demo’s numbers

Each trial produces two outcomes, one for you (A) and one for each candidate (B): ±1 (think “agree/disagree”, “left/right”, etc.). Your outcomes are random.

For the hidden soulmate, B is generated to match A most of the time. Noise flips B with probability = noise. So expected correlation ≈ 1 - 2·noise.

For baseline candidates, B is independent random → expected correlation ≈ 0 (up to sampling wiggles).

The demo “finds the soulmate” by ranking candidates by the observed correlation corr = average(A·B). With enough trials, the linked partner stands out unless noise is too high.

Tip for a write-up: “Entanglement is inferred from correlations; decoherence reduces correlations; no-signaling means local results appear random.”
