A lightweight, link-shareable interactive that teaches entanglement concepts in a fun way.

Welcome to Entangle.Me!

Somewhere in the universe, a small number of people are fundamentally linked to you. Not because of destiny. Not because of romantic algorithms. Because at a deep, quantum level, their states are correlated with yours in a way that can’t be explained away as “just chance.”

  • You don’t know who they are.
  • They don’t know who you are.
  • But when you meet them, something locks into place.
  • You feel it. They feel it. The universe feels it.

Entangle.Me doesn’t create soulmates. It reveals existing quantum correlations.

What you’re seeing (the demo)

This demo does not claim humans are literally quantum-entangled. Instead, it uses the logic entanglement is inferred by in physics: you don’t observe entanglement directly; you infer it from persistent, non-classical correlations.

  • Entanglement (concept): a joint description where outcomes are best explained relationally, via correlation patterns.
  • What it can do: produce correlations that are hard to mimic classically.
  • What it can’t do: transmit a controllable message (each side still looks random on its own).
  • Decoherence: correlations degrade when the “environment” (noise/interference) increases.

In this demo, you (User A) are compared against a pool of candidates. One candidate is secretly the “linked” partner (strong correlation). Others are baseline (chance-like correlation). As trials accumulate, the linked partner rises.

Show the “physics translation” of the demo’s numbers

Each trial produces two outcomes, one for you (A) and one for each candidate (B): ±1 (think “agree/disagree”, “left/right”, etc.). Your outcomes are random.

For the hidden soulmate, B is generated to match A most of the time. Noise flips B with probability = noise. So expected correlation ≈ 1 − 2·noise.

For baseline candidates, B is independent random → expected correlation ≈ 0.

The demo “finds the soulmate” by ranking candidates by the observed correlation corr = average(A·B). With enough trials, the linked partner stands out unless noise is too high.

Thanks for testing my demo! Entanglement is inferred from correlations; decoherence reduces correlations; no-signaling means local results appear random.
